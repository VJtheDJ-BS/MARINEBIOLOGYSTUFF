Our Mission

Understanding Life's Ancient Language

Bioluminescence evolved independently over forty times across the tree of life. In the deep ocean, it is not the exception — it is the rule. Over 76% of marine organisms below 500 metres produce their own light.

At Luminara, we chart this living light: its chemistry, its evolution, and its extraordinary potential for medical diagnostics, sustainable materials, and the fundamental biology of communication itself.

Featured Species

Creatures of Living Light

🪼

200 – 900m

Aequorea victoria

The crystal jelly whose green fluorescent protein revolutionised cell biology and earned the 2008 Nobel Prize in Chemistry.

🦑

600 – 1200m

Vampyroteuthis infernalis

The vampire squid from hell — not a true squid nor octopus — produces complex photophore displays along its entire mantle.

🐠

400 – 800m

Myctophum punctatum

The spotted lanternfish is so abundant it forms the largest biomass migration on earth every night, ascending to feed at the surface.

🦐

1000 – 3000m

Sergestes similis

A mesopelagic shrimp that ejects bioluminescent clouds of mucus as a predator-confusion defence mechanism in the midnight zone.

🐙

800 – 1800m

Watasenia scintillans

The firefly squid of Toyama Bay, Japan — its light displays are used for counter-illumination camouflage against the faint sky above.

🐟

200 – 1000m

Chauliodus sloani

The viperfish dangles a bioluminescent lure from its dorsal fin to attract prey into the reach of its fearsome fang-like teeth.

Timeline

Decades of Discovery

1962

GFP Isolation

Osamu Shimomura first isolates aequorin and GFP from Aequorea victoria, unknowingly starting a biotechnology revolution.

1985

MBARI Deep Surveys

Systematic ROV surveys of the Monterey Submarine Canyon reveal bioluminescence in over 70% of observed organisms below 500m.

2004

Luciferase Gene Atlas

Luminara publishes the first comprehensive atlas of marine luciferase gene families, cataloguing 48 independent evolutionary origins.

2017

Medical Applications

NanoGlow project launches — engineering bacterial luciferase as a real-time tumour-imaging probe for use in surgical oncology.

2024

Hadal Zone Expedition

First confirmed recordings of bioluminescence at 8,900m in the Mariana Trench — the deepest light ever observed in a living organism.
